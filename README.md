-Revenue Performance Analysis Dashboard Report
-Project Overview
This project analyzes the sales performance of Emeka and Sons using an interactive Power BI dashboard. The objective was to evaluate revenue generation, sales representative performance, product contribution, and monthly sales trends to identify key business insights and support data-driven decision-making.
Emeka and Sons required a centralized view of its sales operations to understand overall business performance, identify top-performing products and sales representatives, and uncover patterns influencing revenue growth. The organization needed actionable insights that could improve profitability, optimize inventory planning, and strengthen sales performance.
The primary goal was to analyze sales and revenue data to answer critical business questions:
•	Which products contribute the most to total revenue?
•	Which sales representatives drive the highest revenue?
•	How does sales volume fluctuate throughout the year?
•	Are there operational opportunities to improve performance and reduce business risk?
Using Power BI, a comprehensive revenue dashboard was developed to visualize key performance indicators, including total revenue, order volume, product performance, sales representative contributions, delivery activity, and monthly sales trends.
The analysis involved: Evaluating revenue distribution across products, comparing sales representative performance, identifying seasonal demand patterns, assessing operational efficiency through delivery distribution and Interpreting trends and translating findings into business recommendations.
-Results
The analysis revealed that the business generated ₦32.55 million in revenue from 80 orders and 662 units sold. Product performance analysis showed that Deep Freezers were the dominant revenue driver, contributing approximately 59% of total revenue, making them the company's most valuable product category.
Sales representative analysis identified Blessing Otu as the highest-performing sales representative, generating ₦11.7 million in revenue and significantly outperforming peers. This suggests opportunities to replicate successful sales strategies across the team.
Monthly sales trends revealed strong demand growth leading up to May, which recorded the highest sales volume of the year. Demand declined afterward, then recovered during the December festive period, highlighting clear seasonal purchasing behavior.
The analysis also showed that some representatives generated higher revenue despite handling fewer deliveries, indicating that sales effectiveness and average transaction value have a stronger impact on revenue than transaction volume alone.
-Business Recommendations
Based on the findings, the following recommendations were proposed:
•	Reduce dependency on Deep Freezers by promoting complementary products such as Power Inverters, Solar Panels, and Water Dispensers.
•	Replicate the sales strategies of top-performing representatives through coaching and performance-sharing initiatives.
•	Increase inventory availability and marketing activities ahead of peak demand periods, particularly between April and May and December.
•	Implement targeted promotions and product repositioning strategies for low-performing products.
•	Strengthen cross-selling and upselling efforts to increase average order value and customer lifetime value.
•	Monitor product diversification metrics to reduce revenue concentration risk and improve long-term business resilience.
This dashboard transforms raw sales data into actionable business intelligence, enabling stakeholders to monitor performance, identify growth opportunities, optimize sales strategies, and make informed operational decisions.
